Work out Plan for Beginner

Chest Work out

1.Push-ups

  • Standard Push-Ups: Hands shoulder-width apart, body straight, lower until chest nearly touches the ground.
  • Incline Push-Ups: Hands elevated on a bench or platform.
  • Decline Push-Ups: Feet elevated on a bench or platform.

2.Bench Press

  • Flat Bench Press: Lie flat on a bench and press a barbell up and down.
  • Incline Bench Press: Bench set at a 15-30 degree angle, targeting the upper chest.
  • Decline Bench Press: Bench set at a downward angle, targeting the lower chest.

3. Dumbbell Presses

  • Flat Dumbbell Press: Similar to the flat bench press but with dumbbells.
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: Performed on an inclined bench.
  • Decline Dumbbell Press: Performed on a declined bench.

Shoulder Work Out

1. Shoulder Press

  • Barbell Shoulder Press: Performed with a barbell, pressing it overhead.
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press: Performed with dumbbells, pressing them overhead while seated or standing.

2. Lateral Raises

  • Dumbbell Lateral Raise: Raising dumbbells out to the sides to target the lateral deltoids.

3. Front Raises

  • Dumbbell Front Raise: Lifting dumbbells straight in front of you to target the front deltoids.

Biceps

  • Bicep Curls: Using dumbbells, a barbell, or a cable machine, curl the weight towards your shoulder.
  • Hammer Curls: Similar to bicep curls but with a neutral grip (palms facing each other).
  • Preacher Curls: Performed on a preacher bench to isolate the biceps
  • Concentration Curls: Done seated, focusing on one arm at a time.

Triceps

  • Tricep Dips: Can be done on parallel bars or with your hands on a bench.
  • Tricep Pushdowns: Using a cable machine with a bar or rope attachment.
  • Skull Crushers: Lying on a bench, extend a barbell or dumbbells towards your forehead and back.
  • Overhead Tricep Extensions: Using dumbbells or a cable, extend the weight overhead.

Forearms

  • Wrist Curls: Performed with a barbell or dumbbells, targeting the forearm flexors.
  • Reverse Wrist Curls: Similar to wrist curls but with palms facing down.
  • Farmer's Walk: Holding heavy weights in each hand and walking a set distance.
  • Hammer Strength Curls: Similar to hammer curls but often done with heavier weights or machines.

Upper Back Exercises

  • Pull-Ups/Chin-Ups: Great for overall upper back development.
  • Lat Pulldowns: Focuses on the latissimus dorsi muscles.
  • Bent Over Rows: Targets the middle back and rhomboids.
  • Face Pulls: Works the rear deltoids and upper traps.
  • T-Bar Rows: Effective for the middle of the back.

Lower Back Exercises

  • Deadlifts: A fundamental exercise that works the entire back, including the lower back.
  • Good Mornings: Targets the lower back and hamstrings.
  • Hyperextensions/Back Extensions: Focuses specifically on the lower back muscles.
  • Romanian Deadlifts: Emphasizes the lower back and hamstrings with a slight knee bend.

General Back Exercises

  • Seated Rows: Effective for both the upper and middle back.
  • Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows: Allows for focused work on each side of the back.
  • Kettlebell Swings: Engages the lower back along with other muscle groups.

Leg Work Out

Squats:

  • Bodyweight Squats
  • Goblet Squats
  • Barbell Squats
  • front Squats
  • Bulgarian Split Squats

Lunges

  • Front Lunges
  • Revers Lunges
  • walking lunges
  • Side Lunges
  • Curtsy lunges

Deadlifts

  • Romanian Deadlifts
  • Stiff-Legged Deadlifts
  • Sumo Deadlift
